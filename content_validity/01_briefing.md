## What is AwadhVaani?

**AwadhVaani** ("Voice of Awadh") is a bilingual web-based language-learning app for the **Awadhi** dialect of the Awadh region of Uttar Pradesh, India. It is designed for **young Hindi-speaking learners (ages 15–25)** with little to no prior exposure to Awadhi.

The app teaches Awadhi through six interconnected screens:

| Screen | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Dashboard** | Daily learning goal, XP, streak, Word of the Day, jump-off points |
| **Lessons** | 7 thematic units (Greetings, Family, Food, Daily Life, Nature, Education, Cultural) — each with multiple lessons and example sentences |
| **Quiz** | 4-option MCQ tests of vocabulary, translation, and cultural knowledge |
| **Voice Practice** | Speak Awadhi phrases, get pronunciation feedback via speech recognition |
| **Repository** | Searchable Awadhi-Hindi-English vocabulary with audio playback |
| **Stories** | Folk stories told paragraph-by-paragraph with vocabulary spotlights |

Total content reviewed: **195 items** — 64 vocabulary entries, 59 lesson sentences, 25 quiz items, 47 story paragraphs/vocab.

## What I'm asking you to do

Evaluate whether the **content** of the app is:

1. **Relevant** — important for someone learning Awadhi
2. **Clear** — written / pronounced unambiguously
3. **Authentic** — would actually be used by a native Awadhi speaker (not just Hindi in a different font)

Plus identify:

- Items that are **wrong** (incorrect Awadhi, mistranslated, or mislabeled as Awadhi when actually Hindi)
- **Coverage gaps** — areas of the language that are *missing* from the app entirely

## How to evaluate each item

Open `04_rating_spreadsheet.csv` in Excel or Google Sheets. For each item, fill in four cells:

| Column | Scale | What to consider |
|---|---|---|
| **Relevance** | 1 = Not relevant · 2 = Somewhat · 3 = Quite · 4 = Highly | Is this item important for an Awadhi learner to know? |
| **Clarity** | 1 = Unclear · 2 = Vague · 3 = Clear · 4 = Crystal | Is the Awadhi spelling / romanization / English translation unambiguous? |
| **Authenticity** | 1 = Sounds like Hindi · 2 = Mostly Hindi · 3 = Mostly Awadhi · 4 = Authentically Awadhi | Would a native Awadhi speaker actually use this phrasing? |
| **Essential / Useful / Unnecessary** | Pick one | For Lawshe's CVR: is the item *essential* to the curriculum? |
| **Comments** | Free text | Any specific issue: better Awadhi alternative, wrong tense, sociolinguistic concern, etc. |

You don't have to comment on every item — only the ones that need flagging.
